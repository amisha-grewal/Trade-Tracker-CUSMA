CUSMA — Key Definitions & Context

For CUSMA 2026 review research
Agreement Structure & Terms
CUSMACanada–United States–Mexico Agreement. Canada's name for the 2020 agreement (USMCA in the U.S., T-MEC in Mexico). Replaces NAFTA (1994).
Article 34.7The "joint review" clause — mandates a mandatory review of the agreement by all three parties in 2026 (6 years after entry into force). Either extend for another 16 years or begin sunset proceedings.
Rules of Origin (ROO)Requirements that a product must meet to qualify for preferential tariff treatment. Autos require 75% North American content, with specific steel/aluminium content thresholds.
Rapid Response MechanismUSMCA labor enforcement tool allowing expedited review (as fast as 10 days) of worker rights violations at specific facilities in Mexico. Used over 50 times since 2020.
Chapter 19Binational dispute panel for anti-dumping and countervailing duty cases — a key Canadian priority preserved from NAFTA.
ART vs. FTAAgreements on Reciprocal Trade (new Trump-era tool) are executive-only, not congressionally approved. CUSMA is a congressionally approved FTA — distinct legal standing.
Section 232Trade Expansion Act of 1962 §232 allows tariffs on national security grounds. Steel (25–50%), aluminium (25–50%), and autos (25%) remain active. CUSMA-compliant goods from Canada/Mexico are partially exempt.
MFN RateMost-Favoured-Nation tariff — the default WTO rate applied to all members. Section 122 surcharge (10%) is applied on top of MFN rates post-SCOTUS.
CUSMA Review Priorities (USTR 2026 Agenda)
Trade DeficitU.S. continues to run goods deficits with both Canada and Mexico despite USMCA. USTR 2026 Agenda explicitly flags this as an unresolved problem.
Auto ROOU.S. wants stricter enforcement of the 75% North American content rule and the steel/aluminium wage-rate provisions to prevent tariff avoidance.
Mexico LabourImplementation of Mexico's 2019 labour reform — specifically union democracy and collective bargaining rights — is an ongoing compliance issue under the Rapid Response Mechanism.
Mexico EnergyMexico's preferential treatment of state-owned Pemex and CFE over private/foreign energy investors violates USMCA Chapter 8 (energy) commitments in the U.S. view.
Canada DairyCanada's supply management system (TRQs for dairy, poultry, eggs) — the U.S. claims Canada has not fully honored its USMCA market-access commitments. Won a dispute panel in 2021.
Canada DSTCanada's Digital Services Tax (3% on revenues of large digital companies) — U.S. views as discriminatory against U.S. tech firms and has threatened Section 301 action.
TransshipmentU.S. concern that Chinese goods are being assembled in Mexico to gain USMCA preferential access — especially in autos, steel, and electronics. Key driver of tighter ROO demands.
ISDSInvestor-State Dispute Settlement — the U.S. eliminated ISDS with Canada entirely in USMCA and narrowed it with Mexico. Status in 2026 review is uncertain.
